# Element Quads — Extended Content

> **⚠ EXTENSION NOTE.** Quads are the structural unit Dramatica is built on, but the source term files don't list them — they describe the concept of a Quad and they describe individual Elements, but no file lists the 16 actual Element-Quads. This file fills that gap from Dramatica's published documentation (storymind.com Element charts) and Claude's training knowledge of the theory. Verify against the official Dramatica Story Expert software for canonical placement when building a real storyform.

---

## Why Quads matter for Encoding

A Quad is a 4-element group with three internal pair-relationships:

- **Dynamic pair** (diagonal): the strongest source of conflict
- **Companion pair** (horizontal): mutual support that becomes mutual interference
- **Dependent pair** (vertical): one element implies the presence of the other

When you encode a story moment, you're rarely encoding a single Element — you're activating a Quad. Knowing the surrounding three Elements gives you ready-made dramatic alternatives, character contrast options, and complication sources.

---

## The KTAD Pattern — every Quad is the same fractal

Every Quad in Dramatica's structure is a manifestation of the four foundational forces:

- **K** = Knowledge (what is — the static ground)
- **T** = Thought (what's being considered — the active ground)
- **A** = Ability (what can be done — capacity)
- **D** = Desire (what's wanted — direction)

Mapped at the Class level: Universe=K, Mind=T, Physics=A, Psychology=D. At every other level the same KTAD slots are filled with locally-named Elements, but the structural roles are identical. A Quad's "K-corner" is its anchor; the "D-corner" is its energy/motivation; the "T-corner" is its consideration; the "A-corner" is its enabling capacity.

This is why the same 64 Element names recur in different arrangements at the bottom of each of the four Classes — same atoms, different molecular geometry per Class.

The 64 Elements (16 per Class) group into 16 Quads at the most granular level. These are the **character motivation** quads. The same names recur across Classes in different arrangements.

## How to use the Quads in Encoding

When you've located which Element a story moment activates, the surrounding three Elements in its Quad are your **complication menu**:

1. **Dynamic-pair (diagonal)**: the natural antagonist for this moment. If you encode the dynamic-pair Element on a different character or scene, you create the central conflict-axis.
2. **Companion-pair (horizontal)**: works with this Element until pressure builds — then becomes friction. Useful for the Sidekick/Skeptic relationship dynamic, or for an ally who slowly becomes a complication.
3. **Dependent-pair (vertical)**: this Element implies the other in the same way "lock" implies "key". Encode both, even if only one is foreground.

**Practical example** — if a track or scene revolves around `Test`, its full Quad is `Effect/Trust/Cause/Test`. So:
- The **dynamic-pair to Test** is `Trust` — that's the conflict axis (verse: testing | refrain: trust)
- The **companion to Test** is `Cause` — Test and Cause work together (you test *for* a cause; trying to find the cause leads you to test) until they conflict (the test creates the cause it sought)
- The **dependent of Test** is `Effect` — every test produces an effect; testing implies caring about effects

A bridge in a song using this Quad might escalate from Test (verse) → Cause (build) → Effect (turn) → Trust (resolution).
